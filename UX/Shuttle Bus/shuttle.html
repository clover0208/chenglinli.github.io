Shuttle Bus

Collaborate Project

A mini program helps students
efficiently, time savingly
take school shuttle bus.

This is a school project, because students need to take shuttle bus to and from school and dormitory, the schedule of shuttle bus is not reasonable. Some of the time the bus is empty and some of the time the bus is full and the students can't get on it. We started to find a solution in order to better schedule the shuttle bus.

At first, we think that the problem is that students can’t get the seat on the shuttle bus they want because there aren’t enough seats or busses to accommodate the amount of students. However, to truly solve that problem, it needs the school to provide more shuttle buses and we’ve talked about that with the staff in the public safety. Due to the limit of budget, what we can do is to provide students with updated in time information so that although there are still limited number of seats, it will be easier for students to know when is the time for them to head down if they really want the seat. By doing this, we think it will lessen the frustration of missing a bus and help students maintain their planned schedules.

Problem:
Students cannot get the information in time when they want to take the shuttle bus.

HOW MIGHT WE provide students with the most updated information of the shuttle bus?

In General

After evaluating all the solutions, we decided to apply the mini-program to solve this problem.

Solutions

Stage I (Prototype Version A)


How we come up with the mini-program:

- We come up with this mini-program solution based on the current existing application called “车来了”. It is an application updates the information about the public bus which inspired us to come up with something that can provide students with information about shuttle buses in time.
- As the mini-program format, it is inspired by the current NYUSH Wechat account. We think that if we make it into a mini-program, it can be affiliated with this official account so that it will be easier for students to use.

How we design the interface and functions:

- We considered three aspects of information that students may want to check. First, the most updated information of shuttle buses for each time slot. Second, reserve the seat and cancel the reservation. Third, some relative policies about the reservation system.
- For the updated information, we thought that the number of available seats on each shuttle bus and the number of people paying attention to this time slot could be the information that students want to know the most.
- For reserving the seat, we let students choose their destination first. Then we show them the time slots that are still available. After they choose their wanted time slot, the program will display the seating diagram that shows which seat has been reserved and which is still available. If students click on a reserved seat, there will be a reminder asking them to choose again. If they choose an available seat, there will be a confirmation notification. Click “confirm”, and the seat can be successfully reserved.
- The “cancel seat” button only shows up after they reserved their seats.
- In the “help” button, there are some policies of the reservation system which we use the policy of ARC as our enlightenment. Considering some emergencies of students, we set a deadline of canceling the reservation to 15 minutes before the scheduled time so that other students can still reserve newly empty seats on the bus. There would also be a punishment for late cancelations or disappearance.

How to use the mini-program:

First is the login page (page 1). This is the page showing to the users when they open the mini program in Wechat. Users can click “log in”, then it goes to page 2 where students can log in to the NYU system with their Net ids and passwords in order to reserve a seat. On page 2, they can choose to confirm the login (go to page 3) or cancel (go back to page1). When they go to page 3, they can see their name shown on the upper-left corner of the page. There will be three options shown on this page. One is to check the information about each shuttle bus. If the user clicks on this button, it will go to page 4 which asks the user to choose a destination. After choosing the destination, it will go to page 12 which displays the most updated information about the shuttle bus, including the time slots, available seats and the number of people who are viewing this option. The user can close this page by clicking on the “X” on the top right corner. On the main page (page 3), the second button is “reserve a seat”. When the user clicks on this button, it will go to page 4. On page 4, the user can choose a destination, and then choose a time slot (showing on page5) based on the destination they choose. After that, it will jump to page 6 where they can select seats. If the user clicks on unavailable seats, there will be a notification that kindly reminds them that this seat is not available(page 9). When they choose an available seat, there will be the option to confirm (page 7). If the student clicks on “confirm”, it will go the page 19 where shows the reserved time and the button for cancelation. On the main page (page 3), the third button is “help”. If the user clicks on it, it will go to page 13 where lists the policy of this mini-program. The user can close this page by clicking on the “X” on the top right corner. On page 10, there is a new button called “cancel seat”. If the user clicks it, it will send a notification (page 11). If the user clicks “cancel it”, it will go to page 3 where the user can reserve another seat. If the user clicks “keep it”, it will go back to page 11 and nothing changed. If the user has three late cancellations or disappearances, his account would be blacklisted and his main page will be page 14.

Stage II (Prototype Version B)


What we received from the user test:

- The reserve system is a good idea. It is time-saving for students to check and reserve available seats on the shuttle bus.
- The process of selecting specific seats on the shuttle bus is too complicated and unnecessary. There are too many steps and pages to finish the process of reserving a seat.
-Use different colors to indicate the possibility of successfully reserving a seat on the shuttle bus.
-More punishments for those who reserve the seats but didn’t show up.
-The function of reserving seats in this mini-program is meaningless and complicated because students can still get on the shuttle bus without reservation and it’s embarrassing to kick them off the shuttle bus. Also, students may forget to take their cards with them when getting on the bus.
-Bring the cancellation deadline forward so that students who didn’t reserve a seat successfully can have enough time to check and reserve newly available seats.

How we improve our prototype:

- We change the system to reserve a random seat so that students can feel free to sit wherever they want.
- We combine the “information checking” button and the “reserve seats” button into a “check and reserve” button. When clicking into this button, users can check the information about how many people are viewing this option, how many seats are available on the shuttle bus and reserve their seats.
- We add a waitlist system so that when students cancel their reservations, the students in the waitlist can get the seat.
-We add the function of sending notifications automatically so that it can remind students that their bus is going to set off in 15 minutes.
-We use the current existing equipment which is the card-swiping equipment as the backing up force of our mini-program. Force students to swipe their cards when catching the bus. If they don’t have their cards with them, they should go to public safety for identification.
